Jollof Rice Recipe

Jollof rice is a popular West African dish made with rice cooked in a rich tomato-based sauce, flavored with onions, peppers, and spices. It is often served with chicken, beef, fish, or fried plantains and is enjoyed at parties, family gatherings, and celebrations. Known for its vibrant red-orange color and smoky, savory taste, Jollof rice is considered a beloved cultural staple across countries like Nigeria, Ghana, and Senegal, each with its own variation.

Recipe Information

  1. Preparation time: 20 minutes
  2. Cooking time: 45–60 minutes
  3. Number of servings: 4–6 people
  4. Difficulty level: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2-3 tomatoes, blended or 1 can of tomato paste
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1-2 Scotch bonnet peppers (optional for heat)
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish (optional)

Instructions

Follow these steps to prepare delicious Jollof rice:

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Set aside to drain.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, cooking for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the blended tomatoes or tomato paste, red and green bell peppers, and Scotch bonnet peppers (if using). Cook for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and the oil begins to separate from the tomato mixture.
  5. Add the thyme, curry powder, salt, and pepper to the sauce. Stir well to combine.
  6. Add the drained rice to the pot, stirring to coat the rice with the tomato sauce.
  7. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and simmer for 20-30 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed all the liquid. Avoid lifting the lid too often to prevent steam from escaping.
  8. Once the rice is cooked, fluff it with a fork. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
  9. Garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro if desired. Serve hot with your choice of protein or side dishes.
Additional Tips:
  • For extra flavor, you can add a bay leaf or smoked paprika to the sauce.
  • If you prefer a smokier taste, you can cook the rice over low heat for a few extra minutes to create a slightly crispy bottom layer, known as "party rice."
  • Feel free to customize the recipe by adding vegetables like peas, carrots, or corn for added texture and nutrition.
  • Leftover Jollof rice can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days and reheated on the stovetop or in the microwave.
